# Production Process

## Process Steps

1. **File import/setup** — open the Elliott-supplied production-ready DWG, send to cutter. ~5 min mechanical. NOT billable labor (see `PRICING_RULES.md` §22).
2. **Cut** — Roland SG3-300 on 15" Cardinal Red stock. 1 label across (14.375" height across 15" roll, 0.625" clearance). Single continuous cut path of the "ELLIOTT" wordmark.
3. **Weed** — remove excess vinyl around the 7 block characters. Large simple letters with open counters (E ×3, O ×1) — straightforward weeding, no fine interior detail.
4. **Mask** — apply TransferRite 582U transfer tape over weeded characters. 30" tape allows 2 labels side-by-side per masking pass (2 × 14.375" = 28.75" across 30" tape, 1.25" waste strip).
5. **Inspect** — verify cut accuracy, no lifted edges, character legibility across the 70.8125" length.
6. **Package** — flat or rolled (depending on logistics), masked with transfer tape carrier, ready for hand-application on crane body/boom.

## Production Notes

- **Lamination passes:** 0 (cut vinyl, no print)
- **Cut runs:** 1
- **Equipment:** Roland SG3-300 (15" Cardinal Red stock; 28" max cut width — well within tolerance for the 70.8125" feed length)
- **Weeding complexity:** Low–medium. 7 large block characters at large scale, open counters. Simpler geometry per sq ft than the "E190" / "E160" / "D115" model designations (which are similar-scale characters at smaller overall size). The increased physical scale slows weeding linearly with area, but the open-counter geometry keeps the weeding rate per inch comparable.
- **Cut feed length:** ~76.8" per label (label + 6" spacing). On the 50yd roll (1800" usable), ~23 labels per roll (1800 ÷ 76.8 = 23.4 → 23 complete labels with end waste).

# Pricing Derivation

**Methodology:** Sq ft scaling from the 2.51–2.56 sq ft cluster benchmark (P/N 1205720) with a 19.3% $/sq ft step-down to reflect the structural advantages of a large-format size class. Validated through the full 4-wave AI validation process per `governance/VALIDATION_PROMPTS.md` (24 independent responses across 6 models × 4 waves).

**Per sq ft at qty 20:** $78 ÷ 7.069 = **$11.03/sq ft**

**Step-down from small-format band:** $13.67/sq ft → $11.03/sq ft = **19.3% step-down**. Justified by three structural advantages of the large-format size class:
1. **Fixed labor amortization** — file import, machine setup, inspection, packaging are roughly constant per label regardless of physical size. The fixed-labor share of cost per sq ft drops as label area grows.
2. **Simpler weeding geometry per sq ft** — large block letters with open counters weed faster per sq ft than dense small-format text. The 7-character "ELLIOTT" wordmark with E ×3 and O ×1 open counters is geometrically simpler than a comparable area of small-format model designations.
3. **Lower material cost per sq ft** — $2.83/sq ft on this item vs $3.41/sq ft on the 2.5 sq ft benchmark. The 30" application tape amortizes 2-up against the 15" single-label vinyl feed, reducing tape cost per sq ft by ~$0.08/sq ft.

**Profile Validation:** Establishes the **Large-Format Cut Vinyl band (5+ sq ft)** in the Cut Vinyl Pricing Profile at **$11.03/sq ft at qty 20**. This is a separate band from the existing 2.51–2.56 sq ft concession-phase band ($13.65–$13.94/sq ft). The two bands are independent — they do not contaminate or interact with each other. The January 2027 normalization plan applies to the 2.51–2.56 sq ft cluster only, not to this large-format class.
