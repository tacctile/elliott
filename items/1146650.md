# Nesting and Material Cost

## 90-Degree Rotation Geometry

The label is **40.75" wide × 10.5" tall** when installed. During production the label is **rotated 90 degrees on the roll** so that:

- **10.5" (label height) feeds ACROSS the 24" roll width** — enables 2-across nesting
- **40.75" (label width) feeds ALONG the roll length** — becomes the cut length per row

This is the same length-based, length-feeds-along-roll convention used by every other length-based cut vinyl item on this account (1205720, 3010701, 3010704, 3010707), and matches 3010701's convention exactly.

## Corrected Method — Replaces Round 0 Calculator Brief

The Round 0 calculator brief filed this item at **$10.53/label** using a method inconsistent with the rest of the account: vinyl fed at the raw 40.75" label width with no row-spacing allocation, and tape costed by label area rather than feed length. That method does not match the account-standard length-based nesting convention used on every other wide-format cut vinyl item (see `items/3010701.md`). The corrected calculation below replaces it.

# Production Process

## Process Steps

1. **File import/setup** — open the Elliott-supplied production-ready file, send to cutter. ~5 min mechanical. NOT billable labor (see `PRICING_RULES.md` §22).
2. **Cut** — Roland SG3-300 on 24" Cardinal Red stock. 2 labels across (2 × 10.5" = 21" across 24" roll, 3" clearance). Single continuous cut path of the "40142" model designation, repeated 2-up per row.
3. **Weed** — remove excess vinyl around the "40142" character set. The 40.75" weed span across the row of 2 labels is the dominant time factor.
4. **Mask** — apply TransferRite 582U transfer tape over weeded characters. 24" tape matches 24" vinyl exactly.
5. **Inspect** — verify cut accuracy, no lifted edges, character legibility across the 40.75" width.
6. **Package** — flat, masked with transfer tape carrier, ready for hand-application on equipment.
