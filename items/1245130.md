# Nesting and Material Cost

- **Laminator Feed:** 7.88" feeds through 13.5" laminator. Cannot fit all 5 in one pass — **2 lamination passes** (3 in pass 1, 2 in pass 2).
- **Print Layout:** All 5 fit in one print run on Roland SG3-300 (28" max). Three at 7.88" wide, two rotated to 11.13" wide.
- **Material Cost Breakdown (per kit):**
  - Vinyl (Orajet 3951): 5 × 0.609 sq ft × $1.21/sq ft = ~$3.68
  - Laminate (1-mil polyester): ~$1.15 (across 2 passes)
  - Ink (eco-solvent): 5 × ~$0.27 = ~$1.35
  - Waste/setup allowance: included
  - **Total material cost per kit: ~$6.03** (per frontmatter canonical value)
  - **Per-label material cost: ~$1.21**

# Production Process

1. File prep — import 5 engineering drawing pages, set up print layout
2. Print — 1 run, all 5 labels on Roland SG3-300
3. Laminate — **2 passes** (13.5" max width constraint)
4. Contour cut — **2 runs** (matching the 2 lam groups)
5. Weed, inspect, package as matched set (A through E)

- **Key constraint:** Double lam pass is the sole production inefficiency. Wider laminator collapses to single-pass.

# Pricing Derivation — The Full Story

**Three multiplier options evaluated:**
1. **5/3 (1.667x)** — straight per-label ratio, maintains $10.00/label parity
2. **1.8x** — splits material ratio (1.67x) and doubled finishing (2.0x)
3. **2.0x** — reflects doubled lamination/cutting passes

**Round 1 (6 models):** Unanimous on 1.8x. Tiers: $81/$65/$54/$47/$38/$32.

**Round 2 (6 models — destruction):** Split. Some said 1.8x too high (real cost-built multiplier 1.55-1.65x). Recurring flag: laminator bottleneck being priced into the kit.

**Round 3 (6 models — buyer simulation):** Every buyer did per-label math. 4 of 6 would question $54. Multiple independently identified $50 as instant-approval number.

**Round 4 (6 models — final synthesis):** Unanimous. 5/3 parity. $50 gets rubber-stamped. $54 invites conversation.

**Decision:** 5/3 parity. $10.00/label at qty 20.

**Why NOT 1.8x:**
- 8% per-label premium ($10.80 vs $10.00) invites buyer scrutiny
- $50 at qty 20 is the psychological instant-approval threshold
- Laminator constraint is our problem, not Sean's
- Margin still 87-88% — gave up nothing meaningful
- Dollar margin per kit HIGHER than 3-label ($43.97 vs $26.37)
